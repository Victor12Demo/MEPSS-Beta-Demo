Pathophysiology Overview

Introduction

Pathophysiology is the study of how normal physiological processes are altered by disease. It is the study of structural and functional abnormalities that are expressed as diseases of organs and systems. Pathophysiology considers alterations in function as distinguished from structural defects. The tutorials in this section will offer explanations of the underlying pathophysiology of ten major physiological processes (see below).

  • Aging
  • Blood disorders
  • Cardiovascular circulation
  • Cell injury
  • Fluid and electrolyte imbalance
  • Glandular
  • Immunity
  • Infection
  • Inflammation
  • Oncology
