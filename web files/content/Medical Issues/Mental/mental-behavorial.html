Behavioral characteristics of mental disorders

Introduction

According to ยง4.126 of the Rating Schedule, "when evaluating a mental disorder, the rating agency shall consider the frequency, severity, and duration of psychiatric symptoms". This section will provide information designed to assist the rater in formulating an understanding of psychiatric symptoms and behavioral characteristics, or patterns of behavior, evaluated by examiners when making an assessment of a veteran's mental condition.

For each of the mental disorders listed in the Schedule of Ratings-Mental Disorders ยง 4.130 there are prominent abnormal behavioral characteristics included in the rating criteria found in the General Rating Formula for Mental Disorders. These behavioral characteristics, or patterns of behavior, can be subsumed under major headings related to aspects associated with mental status, such as motor activity, speech patterns, general attitude, emotions (including mood and affect), perceptual anomalies, thought processes, sensorium/cognitive ability, memory, impulse control, and judgement/insight. Included in this section are explanations of these major headings, links to listings of behaviors, and the mental diagnostic codes in which these behaviors apply.

Thought Processes

Thinking activities related to the formulation, organization and expression of ideas that affect language, content and communication.

Disorders of the processes are reflected by factors, such as rate and quantity of thoughts; focus of conversations; rationality of ideas; prevalence of false, fixed beliefs; and preoccupation with stereotyped behaviors and fears.
